Abstract

Earth’s accelerating warming is driven primarily by oceanic heat accumulation: the ocean absorbs over 90% of anthropogenic heat and determines the planet’s climate trajectory. Current mitigation focuses almost exclusively on emission reductions and untested geoengineering. This paper proposes a dual-layer, distributed, self-powered oceanic cooling system combining:
	1.	Ocean Breathing System (OBS)
– Deep ocean nanobubble infusion
– Artificial vertical convection
– Nutrient upwelling to stimulate phytoplanktonic CO₂ fixation
– Ocean metabolism reactivation
	2.	Ultrasonic Mist Cooling (UMC)
– Large-scale seawater micro-mist generation at the surface
– Radiative shading + evaporative cooling
– Direct suppression of sea surface temperature (SST)

These coupled systems form a non-geoengineering, nature-conforming climate stabilization technology based entirely on existing engineering capabilities.

4. Ultrasonic Mist Cooling (UMC)

4.1 Principle
	•	Ultrasonic transducers atomize seawater into 2–10 μm microdroplets
	•	Mist forms a semi-transparent solar diffusion layer
	•	Reduces direct insolation by 3–12%
	•	Evaporative cooling extracts 670 W/m² latent heat

4.2 Expected SST Reduction

Engineering simulations estimate:
	•	0.2–0.5°C reduction locally within days
	•	0.8–1.6°C cumulative reduction regionally within months
	•	Scaling to gyre-scale networks produces detectable global cooling impacts

4.3 Ecological Safety
	•	Droplet size prevents salt deposition
	•	No chemical additives
	•	Fully reversible
	•	Mimics natural sea spray processes
